KOSEN KMITL

The New Generation of Engineering

The KOSEN Institute of King Mongkut's Institute of Technology Ladkrabang (KOSEN - KMITL) was established under the Human Resources Development Project in Engineering, Technology, and Innovation, as outlined in a Cabinet resolution on December 4, 2018. This initiative aims to enhance the capabilities of Thailand's industrial sector and aligns with the Thailand 4.0 policy, particularly targeting the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC). The project focuses on developing skilled human resources in science, technology, and innovation to support both existing and emerging industries, ultimately fostering innovative advancements in the country. The Thai government has partnered with the Japanese government, facilitating investment from foreign companies, including those from Japan, through a government loan project that emphasizes educational assistance. The KOSEN Institute of KMITL operates under the supervision of the Thai-KOSEN Institute, with a minister as the chairman of its executive board, ensuring compliance with the agreements established between the two countries. To align its academic offerings with Japan's KOSEN Institutes, KMITL has entered a cooperation agreement with NIT (National Institute of Technology) Japan. This includes the development of a curriculum that mirrors the Main Core Curriculum (MCC) used in Japan's KOSEN Institutes. As part of this collaboration, NIT is providing expert faculty members to serve as models and assist in training Thai faculty in mathematics, science, engineering, and technology, adhering to the KOSEN educational model from Japan.

Diploma in Engineering

Mechatronics Engineering

(Started in 2019) The Mechatronics Engineering program integrates knowledge from mechanics, electronics, and computer science, with a focus on robotics. It aims to produce and develop smart technologies for industrial applications.

Computer Engineering

(Started in 2021) The Computer Engineering program focuses on the design, development, application, and problem-solving of computer systems and operations in all aspects. It also aligns with global trends in areas such as AI, IoT, and Network & Cyber Security.
